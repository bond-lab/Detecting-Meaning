HG8011: Detecting Meaning with Sherlock Holmes

There are two projects, each worth 20%, with the second building on the first. If you do not do the first, you cannot do the second, and lose 40% of your marks! The due dates for each project are given on the main page.

Project 1: Lexical Semantic Analysis with a Semantic Network (20%)

Annotate (on your own) all open class words in a short section of text, using an updated version of the IMI system (Bond et al, 2015). In this project please do not discuss your annotations with each other.

  • If you have not done so already, read the story (at least up to the part you are assigned, preferably the whole story). This year we will look at the Hound of the Baskervilles
  • Using the on-line tool provided annotate each open class word in a short (roughly 15 sentence) text.
    • For each word you should chose one of the senses in wordnet (Fellbaum 1998) or indicate why this is not possible.
    • There is a quick start guide.
    • More detailed instructions are given on-line, read them.
    • The tool works best under Chrome or Firefox.
    • Your assigned sentences for the first part are here
  • Estimated time 4-6 hours, don't overthink the tags
  • You will be assessed on
    • Completion: is everything tagged
    • Accuracy: are the tags reasonable (do they agree more than a threshold with an expert annotation)
  • If you have tagged everything reasonably accurately then you will pass
  • Comment words where you are not sure of the tag, it will make the second project easier

Project 2: Semantic Analysis — Comparison (20%)

  • You will be given a comparison of your tags with those of other annotators and a merged corpus with the majority tags tagged.
    I will announce when this is ready.
  • You should discuss any differences as a group
  • You should re-tag any words on which there was no majority agreement, or on which you changed your mind.
  • If you mark something with e or w you should add a clear comment. If the same word appears multiple times, you only need to add a comment for the first occurrence.
  • Note that the final annotator is a naive computer — it just tags most frequent sense (mfs)
    • mfs is calculated from the semcor corpus and three Sherlock Holmes Stories (DANC, SPEC and REDH) weighted 1:3 to normalize frequencies
    • Unseen proper nouns are tagged as per
    • Unseen closed class words are tagged as x
    • Unseen monosemous words are tagged with their single sense
    • Other unseen words are tagged None
    • If there are two or more equally frequent senses for a lemma then it is tagged None
  • So feel free to over-ride this annotator!
  • Your assigned sentences for the second part are here
  • Estimated time 4-6 hours. The faster your connection and the bigger your screen, the easier it is.
  • You will be assessed (as a group) on
    • Completion: is everything tagged
    • Accuracy: are the tags reasonable (do they agree more than a threshold with an expert annotation)
  • If you cannot get any discussion from a member of your group, please let me know
